Ten Principles for Bad Design

A calm catalogue of failure: each line names the mistake so the page itself can refuse it.

Bad design is loudest when it forgets that people came to understand something.

01
Hide the purpose.Make the object explain itself only after effort.
02
Add noise.Decorate before the structure has earned attention.
03
Confuse hierarchy.Let captions, titles, and details compete equally.
04
Break trust.Promise one action; deliver another consequence.
05
Ignore use.Design for the photograph, not for the hand or eye.
06
Chase fashion.Let novelty replace proportion, repair, and clarity.
07
Waste material.Spend weight, ink, code, and time without necessity.
08
Flatten character.Remove every particular quality until nothing remains.
09
Refuse honesty.Disguise limits, mechanisms, costs, and intentions.
10
Forget the reader.Let the system admire itself while the message disappears.
