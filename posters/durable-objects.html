The Phantom Hero · 01

Storage records what you wrote. getWebSockets() reports what's reachable. They will disagree.

storage.list("players/")
  • alice@desktop
  • bob@phone
  • dana@web
  • eve@laptop
4  players ready
≠
ctx.getWebSockets()
  • alice@desktop
  • dana@web
2  sockets alive
The fix is three layers, not one. Each closes a different hole — the corpses persist otherwise, the matchmaker keeps feeding the room victims, and you never notice until the support tickets arrive.
A
Reconcile on wake.
In the constructor, diff storage against ctx.getWebSockets() and delete entries that aren't reachable. ~15 LoC.
B
Server heartbeat.
Refresh lastActiveTick on every message. Reap players idle > 2400 ticks (~80 s). Client pings prove the client is alive — only this proves the server can still hear them.
C
Matchmaker prune.
Watch lastStatusChangeAt, not updatedAt. Rooms can churn while frozen in a phantom state. Prune at 10 min stale.

Field Lessons 8 from the war stories
01
setTimeout doesn't survive hibernation. Alarms do.
02
blockConcurrencyWhile lives in the constructor. Never in request handlers — you cap yourself at ~200 req/s.
03
Validate at the Worker. The DO is too expensive to use as a request filter.
04
On e.retryable, throw the stub away and ask for a fresh one. On e.overloaded, do not retry.
05
Sequence numbers must persist. Hibernation resets in-memory state to zero.
06
Server-initiated heartbeats, or they don't prove anything. Client pings only prove the client is alive.
07
Math.random() in render hot paths is a red flag. Determinism is something you build, not something you inherit.
08
wrangler dev or you didn't test. Mocks hide hibernation.
