Ten Principles forBad Design

01
Bad design is old-fashioned for the sake of being old-fashioned.
It distrusts new possibilities and stubbornly clings to outdated forms.
02
Bad design makes a product unusable.
It creates obstacles where clarity and usefulness are needed.
03
Bad design is ugly.
It offends the eye and makes everyday use unpleasant.
04
Bad design makes a product incomprehensible.
It hides its function and forces people to guess.
05
Bad design is intrusive.
It screams for attention and makes itself more important than the person using it.
06
Bad design is dishonest.
It promises more than it can deliver and only pretends to have quality, performance, or meaning.
07
Bad design is short-lived.
It follows fashion, ages quickly, and soon becomes embarrassing.
08
Bad design is careless down to the details.
It treats small decisions as trivial and makes the whole thing worse.
09
Bad design is wasteful.
It consumes materials, energy, attention, and time without good reason.
10
Bad design is as much design as possible.
It adds, overloads, decorates, and hides what matters.
11
Bad design mistakes quantity for quality.
It believes that more features, more decoration, more noise, and more stuff will somehow become better by sheer volume.
