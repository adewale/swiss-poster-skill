01 The Trade / Quiet Exchange

What you traded to fit in.

Normal is a treaty signed on your behalf — a quiet exchange of voltage for permission. The terms, in plain language:

Be agreeable→Be specific
Round the edges→Sharpen them
Hedge the take→Sign the take
Desire paths→Pave a private one
Quote the consensus→Quote the obsession
Be hard to dismiss→Be hard to imitate

03 Patron Saints / Refusenik Index

Famous for the part they refused to fix.

/A — Print

David Carson

Set an interview in Zapf Dingbats because the subject was boring. Ray Gun's house style was its illegibility — and it sold.

"Don't mistake legibility for communication."
/B — Cinema

Werner Herzog

Hauled a steamship over a mountain because the film required it. Ate his shoe to settle a bet about a film he doubted would finish.

"The poet must not avert his eyes."
04 Practices / The Three Moves

How to actually lean.

/A — Subtract
Cut the parts a stranger would have made.

Most "polish" is sanding off fingerprints. Find the moves only you would make and let the rest stay rough. Texture is not a defect.

/B — Confess
Make the obsession the brief.

The thing you've been quietly reading about for ten years is the thing nobody else has read about for ten years. Stop treating it as a hobby.

/C — Refuse the smoothing
Your editor wants to make you legible.

Every system around you is gradient descent toward the median. Add friction. Keep the strange sentence; everything else is negotiable.

05 / Standing Order/05

I will keep the strange sentence. I will ship the version that scares me a little. I will let my work be a fingerprint, not a stencil.
