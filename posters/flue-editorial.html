The Feature Anatomy

A harness is what turns a language model into an agent. Where a software development kit hands you call sites and asks you to wire the loop yourself, Flue gives you a structure you compose: a reasoning core, an environment for it to act in, and the instructions that point it at a goal.

The model thinks; the harness equips it. Around the model sit a sandbox with its own filesystem and shell, sessions that carry memory across turns, the tools it may call, and the skills it loads on demand. You program the harness — not the model — and any model can take the chair.
