A short history · five turns
Even the framework had to re-sense itself.
The record, in order1999 — 2020
1999
Origin · IBM
Dave Snowden builds Cynefin at IBM to map how organisations actually know what they know.
2003
Sense-making named
Kurtz & Snowden publish ‘The new dynamics of strategy’ (IBM Systems Journal): sense-making over categorising.
2007
The four domains go wide
‘A Leader’s Framework for Decision Making’ runs in Harvard Business Review; the four domains reach a wide audience.
2014
The easy domain, renamed
‘Simple’ becomes ‘Obvious’, then ‘Clear’ — the easy domain is where leaders walk off the cliff.
2020
St David’s Day · liminal zones
The St David’s Day model adds liminal zones: the thresholds where a situation is between domains.
The takeaway
The names changed because the world did. A sense-making framework that never revised itself would be the sorting box it warns against.
